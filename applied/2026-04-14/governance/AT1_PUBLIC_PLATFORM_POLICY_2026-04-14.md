# AT1_PUBLIC_PLATFORM_POLICY — Applied Snapshot (2026-04-14)

## Purpose
Define how the public website, interactive experiences, and company-facing layer should be staged inside the governed TFUGA / IA-7 / AT-1 stack.

## Core rule
The public layer must convert governed internal strength into readable, credible, and actionable external entrypoints without inflating claims.

## Three public platform pillars
1. Public website layer
   - mission readability
   - clear TFUGA / IA-7 / AT-1 structure
   - credibility doctrine
   - collaboration entrypoints
2. Interactive experience layer
   - bounded demos
   - cockpit-like exploration
   - simulation or visualization experiences tied to real governed artifacts
3. Company layer
   - AT-1 identity
   - programs and collaborations
   - public-facing proof of method and maturity

## Public law
The site, company, and interactive layers should reveal depth through structure, proof-of-entry, and bounded demonstrators rather than through uncontrolled amplitude.

## Primary outputs
- public site content map
- interactive demo roadmap
- company layer packet
- public platform bundle runner

## Law
The public platform is successful only when it increases conversion, credibility, and clarity at the same time.
