# Real History Ledger v1

## Purpose
This ledger is the canon layer for evidence-backed historical reconstruction in Alexandrie Fractale.

## Rule
Only evidence-backed, source-grounded, and reviewable material belongs here.

## Required fields per item
- id
- title
- civilization_or_region
- time_range
- source_types
- primary_sources
- archaeological_context
- dating_status
- geometry_or_material_notes
- claim_summary
- confidence_status
- citations_required
- review_notes

## Confidence status scale
- established
- strong-plausible
- plausible
- contested
- insufficient

## Canon doctrine
An item enters the Real History Ledger only if it has:
- at least one traceable source family
- context or provenance
- bounded claim wording
- no hidden jump from hypothesis to fact

## Example entries
### RH-001
Title: Pyramid texts as primary funerary textual corpus
Status: established
Claim summary: The Pyramid Texts are a primary textual source for Old Kingdom funerary belief and ritual language.

### RH-002
Title: Monumental Egyptian architecture uses measurable geometric alignment
Status: established
Claim summary: Major Egyptian monumental sites exhibit measurable orientation and geometry that can be documented without overclaiming purpose.

### RH-003
Title: Acoustic properties of enclosed stone chambers may influence sound behavior
Status: plausible
Claim summary: Stone chambers can have notable acoustic behavior, but functional intent must not be asserted without stronger evidence.

## Output use
This ledger feeds:
- the Established History layer on the site
- the evidence rail in the educational game
- the anti-inflation review gates

## Final law
The Real History Ledger is not where the system dreams. It is where the system earns the right to speak precisely.
